KingVan Wins Major European Order for Automated Laser Welding Stations

June 24, 2026

JINAN, China – KASSEL, Germany – KingVan Technology has secured its largest single order to date from a leading European window profile extruder, contracting for 12 automated fiber laser welding stations to be deployed across the customer's production facilities in Germany, Poland, and Spain. The order, valued at approximately EUR 3.8 million, covers the complete KV-LW3000 laser welding systems including integrated robotic part handling, vision-based seam tracking, and real-time quality monitoring.

The European manufacturer, one of the top five thermal break aluminum window producers in the EU, selected KingVan's automated laser welding solution after a rigorous 12-month evaluation that included on-site pilot production comparing laser welding against the customer's existing MIG welding lines. The trial results showed that KingVan's system delivered 40% faster cycle times, 60% reduction in post-weld finishing labor, and a 25% reduction in overall frame assembly cost per unit.

"This order represents a significant milestone for KingVan in the European market," said the company's director of international operations. "European window manufacturers have historically been conservative in adopting new joining technologies, preferring established MIG and TIG methods. The fact that a major EU extruder is now standardizing on laser welding across multiple plants signals a genuine shift in the industry's technology trajectory. We expect this to open doors with other European producers facing the same capacity and labor cost pressures."

System Configuration and Integration

Each KV-LW3000 station is configured as a turnkey work cell comprising a 3-kilowatt single-mode fiber laser source, a six-axis collaborative robot for part manipulation, a two-axis gantry for the laser welding head, and KingVan's proprietary L-Weld Vision system for real-time seam tracking. The system is capable of welding 6063-T5 and 6060-T66 aluminum profiles with 1.5 mm to 4 mm wall thickness at speeds of 3 to 6 meters per minute.

The vision integration is a key differentiator: the seam tracking camera captures the joint geometry at 200 frames per second and adjusts the laser beam position within a ±2 mm compensation range at full welding speed. This allows the system to accommodate the normal dimensional variations in extruded aluminum profiles without requiring precise mechanical fixturing. For the customer's product mix of over 200 distinct window profile designs, this eliminates the need for product-specific welding fixtures, reducing changeover time between product runs from 45 minutes to under 5 minutes.

Quality Monitoring and Data Integration

Each welding station is equipped with an integrated optical coherence tomography (OCT) sensor that measures weld penetration depth and seam geometry in real time. The OCT data, combined with laser power, welding speed, and shielding gas flow rate, is logged to a central manufacturing execution system (MES) for full traceability. In the event that weld parameters drift outside the predefined process window, the system automatically pauses production and alerts the operator, preventing defect generation.

The customer's quality director commented: "Traceability is increasingly important for our clients in the commercial building sector, where window performance certifications require documented proof of manufacturing process control. KingVan's OCT-based quality monitoring gives us exactly that — a per-joint digital record of weld quality that we can share with our clients and third-party certifiers."

Deployment Timeline and Service Support

The first four KV-LW3000 systems are scheduled for delivery to the customer's German facility in October 2026, with the remaining eight stations to be installed across the Polish and Spanish plants by March 2027. KingVan has established a dedicated European service center in Frankfurt with a team of five field service engineers to provide on-site support and spare parts logistics for the installed base. The service team will also manage the two-year comprehensive warranty included in the contract, covering all laser source and optics components.

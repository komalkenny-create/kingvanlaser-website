ASEAN Window Manufacturers Adopt UV Laser Marking at Record Pace as Green Building Standards Tighten Across Southeast Asia

July 15, 2026

JINAN, China – Window and door profile manufacturers across the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) are adopting UV laser marking technology for polyamide thermal break strip traceability at an unprecedented pace, driven by the rapid expansion of green building certification programs and national energy efficiency mandates across Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. KingVan Technology reports that KV-UV series laser marking system sales to ASEAN extrusion plants grew 120% year-on-year in the first half of 2026, surpassing even the company's growth rate in the European market during the same period.

The acceleration reflects a fundamental shift in the ASEAN construction market. Thailand's updated Building Energy Code (BEC), effective January 2026, now mandates minimum thermal performance standards for window and curtain wall systems in all new commercial buildings over 2,000 square meters. Vietnam's QCVN 09:2026, published in March, establishes the country's first comprehensive standard for building envelope thermal performance, while Indonesia's Green Building Council has expanded its GREENSHIP certification program to require material traceability documentation—including permanent marking of thermal break components—for the highest certification tiers.

"Southeast Asia has reached an inflection point in building envelope regulation that mirrors what we observed in Europe in 2023–2024," said KingVan's director of Asia-Pacific operations. "Three years ago, UV laser marking for thermal strips was essentially unknown in the ASEAN extrusion industry. Today, we are installing multi-system orders at plants in Rayong, Haiphong, Bekasi, and Johor Bahru, and the pipeline of inquiries from regional extruders has grown fivefold since the start of 2025. The market is moving from early adoption to mainstream adoption faster than any region we have tracked."

Market Drivers: Regulation, Exports, and Quality Standards

Three interconnected factors are driving the ASEAN adoption of UV laser marking for thermal break strips:

  • Domestic regulatory pressure: Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia now require all polyamide thermal break strips used in certified green buildings to carry permanent marks identifying the manufacturer, material grade (PA66, PA66 GF25, or PA66 GF30), production date, and batch number. These requirements parallel the traceability provisions of the European EN 14024 standard and were in fact modeled on them, with technical assistance from the European Committee for Standardization.
  • Export market access: ASEAN-based window profile extruders are increasingly exporting finished products to the European Union, Japan, and Australia—all of which impose permanent marking requirements on thermal break components. A major Thai extruder with 40% export exposure to the EU has standardized exclusively on KingVan's KV-UV15 system across its 16 polyamide extrusion lines, citing the need to meet EN 14024 traceability requirements for its European customers.
  • Quality assurance and brand protection: Thermal break strip counterfeiting has emerged as a significant quality issue in fast-growing ASEAN construction markets. Permanent UV laser marks provide an irrefutable authentication mechanism that enables builders, inspectors, and certification bodies to verify the origin and material specification of thermal break strips at any point in the supply chain. KingVan reports that 60% of its new ASEAN customers cite anti-counterfeiting as a primary motivation for adopting laser marking, alongside regulatory compliance.

Technology Adoption Patterns in ASEAN Markets

KingVan's deployment experience across ASEAN has revealed distinct adoption patterns that reflect the region's diverse market structure. In Thailand and Vietnam, where large-scale extrusion plants with 10 to 20+ production lines dominate the market, the preference is for the company's KV-UV15 high-speed model, capable of marking 150 thermal strips per minute with 0.1 mm positional accuracy. These systems are typically integrated directly into the extrusion line's puller section, enabling marking to occur in-line without a separate production step.

In Indonesia and the Philippines, where the extrusion industry is more fragmented with numerous medium-sized producers operating three to eight lines each, KingVan has seen stronger demand for its KV-UV10 compact model. The KV-UV10 offers the same UV laser source technology at a lower throughput (80 strips per minute) but at a 35% lower capital cost, making it accessible to smaller extruders. KingVan has also introduced a localized service program for the ASEAN market under which application engineers based in Bangkok, Ho Chi Minh City, and Jakarta provide on-site installation, training in local languages, and preventive maintenance services.

A notable trend has been the adoption of KingVan's MarkOS software platform among ASEAN extruders. The software's ability to pre-configure labeling templates for multiple regulatory regimes—including Thailand's BEC, Vietnam's QCVN 09, Indonesia's GREENSHIP, and export-market standards such as EU EN 14024 and Australia's AS 2047—has proven valuable for plants serving both domestic and export markets. "Our MarkOS software was originally designed to simplify compliance for European customers facing a multi-standard environment," the Asia-Pacific director noted. "In ASEAN, that same multi-standard flexibility is now the feature that customers cite most frequently as a deciding factor in system selection."

Regional Supply Chain and Growth Outlook

To support its expanding ASEAN customer base, KingVan has established a spare parts and consumables depot in Bangkok's Lat Krabang Industrial Estate, serving customers across Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos. A second depot in Batam, Indonesia, is scheduled to open in Q4 2026, covering the Indonesian and Malaysian markets. The company has also certified three local service partners in Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia to perform warranty repairs and preventive maintenance, reducing service response times from an average of two weeks (from China) to under 48 hours.

Looking ahead, KingVan expects ASEAN to become its fastest-growing regional market for UV laser marking systems in 2027, with projected revenue growth of 80 to 100% driven by continued regulatory tightening, expanding export demand, and increasing awareness of laser marking's quality assurance and anti-counterfeiting benefits. The company will showcase its ASEAN-focused product line at Architect Expo 2027 in Bangkok (April 27–May 2, 2027) and at VietBuild 2027 in Ho Chi Minh City (June 16–20, 2027).

"The ASEAN market today reminds me of the European market in 2024—the regulatory framework is falling into place, the early adopters have proven the technology, and the rest of the industry is preparing to follow," KingVan's Asia-Pacific director said. "For KingVan, being embedded in this transition from the beginning positions us to be the long-term marking-technology partner for a region that will define the next decade of the global thermal break strip industry."

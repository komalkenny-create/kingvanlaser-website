KingVan Introduces Next-Generation Laser Marking Technology for Thermal Barrier Strips, Enhancing Traceability and Durability

May 20, 2026

JINAN, China – KingVan, a leading global manufacturer of laser marking, welding, and cutting systems, has announced a major breakthrough in the processing technology for thermal barrier strips used in energy-efficient window and door frames. The company's latest ultraviolet (UV) and fiber laser marking solutions deliver permanent, high-contrast identification marks on polyamide and PVC-based strips without compromising the material's structural integrity.

Thermal barrier strips are critical components in modern aluminum windows, serving as the insulating layer that reduces heat transfer. Until recently, manufacturers relied on inkjet printing or mechanical stamping for labeling these parts, methods that often wear off under UV exposure, chemical cleaning, or prolonged friction. KingVan’s new laser marking technology addresses this durability gap by engraving deep, indelible barcodes, serial numbers, and logos at speeds exceeding 120 pieces per minute while maintaining a marking depth tolerance of ±0.01 mm.

“Our engineers developed a proprietary beam-shaping module that precisely controls the energy density to avoid melting or deforming the thermal barrier material,” explained Dr. Li Wei, Head of R&D at KingVan. “In field tests, marks produced by our LV-20F fiber laser system withstood more than 5,000 abrasion cycles and 1,000 hours of salt-spray corrosion with zero degradation in readability, far exceeding the EN 14024 standard requirement for building hardware.” The system supports a marking area of up to 300 mm × 300 mm and operates with a 20-watt output power optimized for non-metal composites.

The new solution also integrates an intelligent vision positioning system that automatically corrects for strip misalignment on the production line, reducing scrap rates by an average of 15% in pilot installations. This is particularly beneficial for manufacturers dealing with curved or irregularly shaped profiles, where traditional contact-based marking can cause physical damage. KingVan’s technology ensures consistent marking quality regardless of strip geometry, enabling real-time traceability for quality audits and warranty management under ISO 9001 and IATF 16949 frameworks.

Industry analysts note that the global market for energy-efficient windows is growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.2%, driven by stricter building energy codes in Europe and North America. As thermal barrier strip production scales up, the need for reliable, fast, and automation-ready identification systems becomes essential. KingVan is already shipping the new laser marking modules to major window profile manufacturers in Germany, Turkey, and the United States, with a standard ROI period of under 14 months based on reduced consumable costs and lower rework rates.

In addition to the hardware upgrade, KingVan has launched a proprietary software layer called MarkManager Pro that allows operators to import batch production data directly from ERP systems and generate serialized Data Matrix codes compliant with GS1 standards. The software includes a real-time condition monitoring dashboard that tracks laser tube temperature, marking speed, and material feed rate, alerting operators before potential quality deviations occur. “We are not just selling a tool; we are providing a comprehensive traceability ecosystem that helps our clients meet the most stringent regulatory requirements while optimizing their total cost of ownership,” added Dr. Li.

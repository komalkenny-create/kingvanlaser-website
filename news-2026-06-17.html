KingVan Launches Handheld Laser Cleaning System for Aluminum Extrusion Die Maintenance

June 17, 2026

JINAN, China – KingVan Technology has introduced the KV-LC2000, a 2-kilowatt handheld fiber laser cleaning system designed specifically for aluminum extrusion die maintenance. The new system removes aluminum oxide, residual metal, and carbon deposits from extrusion die bearing surfaces in under three minutes per die, reducing die cleaning downtime by 80% compared to conventional chemical bath and manual abrasion methods.

"Die cleaning and maintenance is one of the most labor-intensive and hazardous operations in any aluminum extrusion facility," said the KingVan product development team lead. "Traditional methods require workers to handle caustic sodium hydroxide solutions at elevated temperatures, followed by manual wire brushing—a process that takes 15-30 minutes per die and exposes operators to serious chemical and ergonomic hazards. The KV-LC2000 eliminates chemicals entirely and reduces cleaning time to a fraction of current methods."

Precision Cleaning Without Die Surface Damage

The KV-LC2000 employs a pulsed fiber laser source operating at 1064 nm with adjustable pulse energy and repetition rate, giving operators precise control over the cleaning depth. For aluminum extrusion dies made of H13 tool steel, the system can be tuned to remove aluminum oxide layers (up to 50 microns thick) without affecting the underlying die steel, which has a higher ablation threshold. The adjustable beam scanning pattern allows the operator to cover the complex contours of the die bearing surface uniformly, including sharp internal corners and narrow slots common in multi-cavity dies.

Field testing at a partner extrusion plant in Guangdong province demonstrated that dies cleaned with the KV-LC2000 produced extrusion profiles with surface finish quality equivalent to dies cleaned with chemical methods, with zero measurable change in die dimensions after 30 cleaning cycles. By contrast, chemical cleaning methods showed progressive etching of the die steel at a rate of 2-5 microns per cycle, meaning chemical-cleaned dies required reconditioning after 15-20 cycles, while laser-cleaned dies maintained dimensional integrity through 30 cycles and counting.

Quantified Productivity Gains

In production trials, the KV-LC2000 delivered the following improvements over conventional chemical cleaning methods:

  • Cleaning time per die: 2.5 minutes (laser) vs. 20 minutes (chemical + manual brushing)
  • Chemical consumption: Zero vs. 2-4 liters of caustic solution per cleaning
  • Operator exposure: No chemical handling vs. mandatory PPE for NaOH handling
  • Die life between reconditioning: 30+ cleanings vs. 15-20 cleanings
  • Annual die maintenance cost (per 50 dies): Estimated USD 8,500 (laser) vs. USD 22,000 (chemical + reconditioning)

For a medium-sized extrusion plant running 50 active dies with daily cleaning requirements, the KV-LC2000 delivers a projected payback period of 8-12 months, depending on local labor and chemical disposal costs.

Built for Factory Floor Duty

The KV-LC2000 is housed in a compact mobile cart measuring 800 mm by 600 mm by 1,200 mm, with pneumatic locking casters for stable positioning at the die prep station. The laser head weighs 3.2 kg, including a 5-meter fiber delivery cable, and features a pistol-grip design with an integrated safety interlock trigger. The system includes a 1,500-watt fume extractor with HEPA and activated carbon filtration to capture ablation byproducts, meeting workplace exposure limits for metal oxide particulates.

The system is now available for order with a lead time of 4-6 weeks. KingVan provides on-site operator training as part of the commissioning package and offers extended warranties covering the laser source for up to 30,000 operating hours. The KV-LC2000 will be demonstrated at the KingVan showroom in Jinan and at select industry trade shows through Q3 2026.
